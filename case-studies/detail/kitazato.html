Kitazato put their shoes in the booth display

Kitazato is a Japan-headquartered global leader in assisted reproductive technology, founded in 1996. The company is best known for developing the Cryotop Method, the dominant global technique for vitrification of human eggs and embryos. With 550+ products across the IVF cycle, Kitazato shows up at every major reproductive medicine conference.

Challenge

And the team had a hard early-October trade show deadline. Sample first, design tweaks, expedited production. No margin for error. The conference date does not move, and the booth either has the shoes on opening day or it does not.

The project landed on Scott Brown of Kitazato's US team, who picked the Zero Canvas silhouette in iconic Cryotop blue. The wrong shade of blue would read as off-brand at the same glance an embryologist makes when walking past the booth. The shoes needed to be wearable on a busy conference floor and presentable in a glass display case, looking like Kitazato at a distance and feeling right in close-up. There was no version of this that could be 'good enough.'

Kitazato wanted differentiation that didn't add more flyers, more giveaways, or more booth structure. Most branded swag ends up in a drawer at home and is never seen again. The company was looking for something that could live alongside its flagship medical products, not as a giveaway to be discarded but as a brand artifact worth keeping. The constraint was clinical, not loud. Kitazato's identity in the IVF market is premium and precise, and any swag had to read the same way.

Trade show floors at IVF conferences are crowded. ASRM, MRSi, PEARLS Society, ESHRE bring dozens of vendors filling the room with handouts, pop-up signs, and giveaway tables. Embryologists and clinic directors walk those floors with limited time and infinite options. The vendors that get noticed are the ones that look different at a glance, not the ones that hand out one more flyer.

Solution

Scott looked at the finished pairs and made the call to put a pair in the display case. The shoes did not need explaining at the booth. They sold the brand at a glance, and the booth team did not have to add a single line of pitch around them. "Shipping was a breeze," Scott said in a video he recorded after the event. "They put them right to the hotel for us. It couldn't have been any easier." He sent the video to Shoe Zero unprompted, on his phone.

Two staging strategies came out of one order. The rep team got pairs sized to walk the conference floor, turning every Kitazato sales presence into a walking brand asset before a flyer ever changed hands. A second pair was reserved for the booth's glass display case, treated as a premium artifact alongside the company's flagship medical products.

The design moved fast through sample, design tweaks, and expedited production to make the early-October trade show debut. Logo placement, brand-color discipline, and the on-foot finish were locked in before production. The brief was specific from the start: shoes good enough to wear and to display.

Results

The shoes made the flagship display

Kitazato gave the custom shoes their own spot in the booth's glass display case, alongside the company's most-discussed product lines. Most branded swag ends up in a drawer or on a giveaway pile. Kitazato's ended up under glass, treated as a premium brand artifact rather than a promotional throwaway. The display case is the company's curated lineup of what they want clinic directors to look at, and the shoes earned the same treatment without a sales pitch. A clinical-grade IVF brand putting shoes next to flagship medical products is its own endorsement, before a customer ever speaks.

Conversations started before the booth did

On the conference floor, Kitazato's team walked the show wearing their custom Cryotop-blue shoes. Conversations started before reps even reached the booth. "They were a huge hit. Customers went nuts," Scott recalled after the event. The team did not have to flag down attendees with a giveaway. Custom sneakers in iconic Cryotop blue did the visual work for the brand, the team carried that signal across the floor, and the booth conversation picked up from somewhere already warm.

Custom shoes became how Kitazato shows up

What started as a single trade show order became an ongoing partnership across multiple events and design iterations. Kitazato has built custom shoes into how the company shows up at IVF conferences worldwide. ASRM and MRSi both see the same brand identity walk in, in shoes that weren't part of the booth presence three years ago. The first team sample was placed in September 2023. The full team rollout followed in October. Each event since has refined the design and renewed the order. PEARLS Society and ESHRE picked up the same Cryotop-blue silhouette at their respective conferences. Three years of shoes is not a one-event marketing experiment. It is a recurring brand-presence line item that does not get cut from the budget the way one-off swag does.
